A pub, situated close to Teignmouth seafront, that has changed its name back and forth for some years, being traditionally the Dawlish Arms, then Dicey O'Reillys, than the Dawlish Arms again and appears now to have settled on Dicey Reillys. A nicely and gently themned Irish Pub well run by Dominic and Ann-Marie Conroy. It has but one Real Ale pump, along with all the Guiness and the Caffrey. However they do concentrate on a local micro-brewery and the ale is kept in great condition and at the right temperature. This is a very comfortable and welcoming pub, with good food and a good attitude. Well worth a visit.

Visited this Irish-style pub on the 15/6/2011. Though it only has one Real Ale on draught it was well kept and a good temperature. One hopes that the landlord Dominic Conroy may be persuaded to add another Real Ale pump in time.....perhaps even giving an opportunity to the growing number of Irish micro-breweries to reach a British market.
